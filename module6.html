What is Process Engineering?

Process engineering is the discipline of studying how work currently gets done and using AI to make it better. While prompt chaining improves your prompts and context engineering improves your inputs, process engineering improves your entire workflow.

This is where vibe coding goes from "I can build a website" to "I can transform how an entire team operates."

Step 1: Observe & Record

Before you can improve a process, you need to understand it deeply. The best way to do this is to watch it happen.

Techniques for Observation

  • Screen Recording ‚Äî Record someone performing their workflow. Tools: Loom, OBS, native screen recording. Capture every click, every decision, every hesitation.
  • Screenshare Walkthroughs ‚Äî Have someone walk you through their process live while narrating their decisions. Ask "why?" at each step.
  • Process Documentation ‚Äî Write down each step in sequence. Note time spent, tools used, handoffs, and pain points.
  • Shadow Sessions ‚Äî Sit beside someone (or on a video call) and observe them work without interrupting. Take notes on friction points.
üí° Pro Tip

Record the process before you start optimizing. This gives you a baseline to measure improvement against, and the recording itself becomes context you can feed to AI.

Step 2: Analyze & Identify Opportunities

Once you've documented the process, look for these patterns:

üî¥ Bottlenecks

Steps that take disproportionately long or create queues. Example: "Reviewing 50 applications manually takes 3 hours every week."

üü° Repetitive Tasks

Anything done the same way every time. Example: "Copy data from this spreadsheet into that form, every morning."

üü† Error-Prone Steps

Steps where humans frequently make mistakes. Example: "Formatting email addresses incorrectly 15% of the time."

üîµ Manual Handoffs

Places where work passes between people or tools and things get dropped. Example: "Saving a PDF, then emailing it, then logging it in the CRM."

Feed Your Process to AI
"Here's a process my team follows every week: [paste process documentation]. Analyze this workflow and identify: 1) The biggest bottleneck, 2) Steps that could be automated, 3) Where AI could add the most value, 4) A proposed improved workflow."

Step 3: Build & Automate

Now use your vibe coding skills to build the solution:

Common Solutions

  • Dashboards ‚Äî Vibe code a dashboard that replaces manual data gathering
  • Workflows ‚Äî Build automation chains using tools like n8n, Make, or Zapier
  • Internal Tools ‚Äî Create custom apps for specific team needs
  • Templates & Generators ‚Äî Build tools that produce documents, emails, or reports from templates
  • Chatbots & Assistants ‚Äî Create AI assistants trained on team knowledge
Example: Vibe Coding an Automation
"Build me a web app that: 1) Accepts a CSV upload of customer data, 2) Validates email formats and flags errors, 3) Deduplicates entries by email, 4) Generates a formatted report showing clean vs flagged entries, 5) Exports a cleaned CSV for import into our CRM."

The Continuous Improvement Loop

Process engineering isn't a one-time activity. It's a continuous cycle:

1. Observe ‚Üí Build

  • Record the current process
  • Identify the biggest bottleneck
  • Build a solution with AI tools
  • Deploy and train the team

2. Measure ‚Üí Improve

  • Track time saved, errors reduced
  • Gather feedback from users
  • Observe the new process
  • Identify the next bottleneck
üîÑ The Key Insight

Every time you improve a process and remove a bottleneck, a new one becomes visible. The goal isn't perfection ‚Äî it's continuous, compounding improvement.

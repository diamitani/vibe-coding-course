Prompt Chaining

The difference between mediocre and exceptional AI output usually comes down to one thing: the quality of your prompt. Prompt chaining is the technique of using one AI conversation to refine and improve prompts before sending them to your builder tool.

  • What Is It
  • Why It Works
  • Chain Patterns
  • Live Examples
  • Practice

What is Prompt Chaining?

Prompt chaining is the practice of using one LLM to craft, refine, or enhance the prompt you'll send to another tool. Instead of going directly from your rough idea to a builder, you add a "refinement" step in between.

Your Rough Idea
"I want a red background"
‚Üì Ask an LLM to refine this
Refined Prompt
"Set the background to a deep crimson red using hex color #DC143C. Apply a subtle radial gradient that transitions from #DC143C at center to #8B0000 at the edges. Add a very faint noise texture overlay at 3% opacity for depth."

The refined prompt is dramatically more specific. AI builder tools perform much better with this level of detail because they don't have to guess what you mean.

Why Prompt Chaining Works

  • Eliminates ambiguity ‚Äî "Red" could mean 100 different shades. The chain forces specificity.
  • Adds expert knowledge ‚Äî The refining LLM adds best practices you might not know (gradients, fallback colors, accessibility).
  • Saves iteration cycles ‚Äî Better first output means fewer "no, not like that" follow-ups.
  • Works across domains ‚Äî Design, copy, architecture, data models ‚Äî everything benefits from clearer prompts.

The Four-Step Chain Pattern

The most effective prompt chains follow this pattern:

  1. 1. Clarify

    Take your rough idea and ask an LLM to identify what's ambiguous. "What questions would you ask to make this prompt more specific?"

  2. 2. Specify

    Feed the answers back and ask the LLM to generate a detailed, specific version of your prompt.

  3. 3. Generate

    Send the refined prompt to your builder tool (Lovable, Bolt, v0, etc.).

  4. 4. Validate

    Review the output. If it's not right, chain again ‚Äî describe what's wrong and let the LLM adjust the prompt.

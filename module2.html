What is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is the practice of building software by describing what you want in natural language and letting AI generate the code. It's a fundamental shift from writing code to directing AI ‚Äî and it's reshaping who can build technology.

  • Origin Story
  • How It Works
  • Use Cases

Origin: Where the Term Came From

The term "vibe coding" was coined by Andrej Karpathy, former Director of AI at Tesla and co-founder of OpenAI, in a widely shared post in early 2025:

Andrej Karpathy
"There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding', where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists. It's not really coding ‚Äî I just see stuff, say stuff, run stuff, and copy paste stuff, and it mostly works."

Karpathy captured something millions of people were already doing: using AI assistants to build real software without manually writing traditional code. The "vibe" part means you focus on the outcome ‚Äî what you want to exist ‚Äî and let AI handle the implementation details.

How Vibe Coding Works

At its core, vibe coding follows a simple loop:

  1. Describe ‚Äî Tell the AI what you want in natural language
  2. Generate ‚Äî The AI produces code, designs, or functionality
  3. Review ‚Äî See the result (usually a live preview)
  4. Refine ‚Äî Adjust with follow-up prompts until it matches your vision

The Mindset Shift

Vibe coding requires a different way of thinking than traditional development:

Traditional Developer

  • Thinks in syntax and APIs
  • Writes code line by line
  • Solves problems through code
  • Values technical expertise
  • Builds bottom-up

Vibe Coder

  • Thinks in outcomes and descriptions
  • Writes prompts and refines results
  • Solves problems through communication
  • Values clarity and specificity
  • Builds top-down
‚úÖ Key Takeaway

The best vibe coders aren't necessarily the best programmers. They're the best communicators ‚Äî people who can clearly and specifically describe what they want to create.

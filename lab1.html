Step-by-Step Walkthrough

  1. Prepare Your Context Documents

    Before touching any builder tool, create a mini PRD and design system using the templates from Module 5. Define your product name, color palette, typography, and voice.

  2. Chain Your Initial Prompt

    Write a rough description of your marketing site. Then use prompt chaining (Module 4) to refine it into a detailed, specific prompt. Include colors, fonts, section layout, and copy direction.

  3. Generate v1 with Your Builder

    Paste your refined prompt into your chosen tool (Lovable, Bolt, v0, etc.) and let it generate the first version. Don't worry about perfection yet.

  4. Review & Iterate

    Look at the output critically. What's wrong? What's missing? Use follow-up prompts to fix specific issues. Be precise: "Change the hero background gradient from blue to purple (#8B5CF6 ‚Üí #6D28D9)."

  5. Add Polish

    Request animations, hover states, responsive adjustments, and micro-interactions. These are what separate amateur from professional output.

  6. Test & Deploy

    Test on mobile (use browser DevTools to simulate). Check all links, buttons, and interactions. Deploy via your tool's built-in hosting or services like Vercel/Netlify.

üí° Hint

The best projects in this lab will demonstrate clear evidence of prompt chaining (before/after prompts) and context engineering (PRD + design system provided to the tool).

Completion Checklist

  • Hero section with headline, subheadline, and CTA
  • At least 3 feature cards with descriptions
  • Social proof section (testimonials, stats, or logos)
  • Pricing section with at least 2 tiers
  • Responsive design (works on mobile and desktop)
  • Consistent color palette and typography
  • At least 2 hover effects or animations
  • Footer with navigation links
  • Site is deployed and accessible via a public URL
